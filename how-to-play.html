How to Play

At first glance, the slot is easy to understand: five reels, three rows and twenty fixed paylines. The real edge comes from knowing where the premium features live and when a session shifts from base play into bonus hunting.

Your goal is not just to spin fast. You want to read reels two to four carefully, because that is where multiplier wilds and the most meaningful momentum changes appear.

What to watch on every spin

Wilds only appear on reels two, three and four, and every one of them matters because they can arrive with 2x or 3x multipliers. When the 3x3 Colossal Wild lands, normal line hits can instantly become headline moments.

Free spins are where the slot separates itself from the original Dog House. A bonus wheel can award up to 27 spins, sticky wilds stay active, and Biggie or Ghost Out can completely reshape how the round behaves.

  • Wins are paid across 20 fixed paylines, so symbol position still matters more than raw screen count.
  • Wild multipliers only spawn on reels two, three and four, making the middle reels the true engine room.
  • Three scatters trigger the feature. If the base game feels flat, the session can still flip in one strong wheel result.

Session notes

  • Start with a stake that covers at least 150 to 200 spins if you plan to play organically.
  • Raise your bet only after a clear profit buffer, not because a cold stretch feels overdue.
  • If you use Ante Bet, cut session length expectations in advance because spend accelerates noticeably.
  • Treat a strong Biggie or Ghost Out bonus as a valid exit point rather than automatically recycling the win.
